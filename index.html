Education

How AI search actually works

"AI does not cite websites. It cites chunks of knowledge."
  • LLMs process text in ~400 word chunks (approximately 512 tokens). Each chunk must be self-contained and clearly structured to be retrieved and used in an answer
  • AI performs query fan-out. One user query triggers multiple sub-searches simultaneously. From the AirOps study: 89.6% of queries trigger follow-up searches, ~37 pages retrieved per query, but only ~5 are cited
  • Headings create contextual anchors. LLMs use heading structure to interpret what a section is about. Structured formatting helps AI understand relationships and intent
  • You are competing with content sections, not entire pages. AI answers pull the best chunk, not the best page. If your content is structured clearly, your brand is more likely to be found and cited

Content Structure

What content needs to look like for AI

The shift from "How do we rank this page?" to "How will AI retrieve this answer?"

Single clear H1

One topic per page with an entity-first introduction that answers the core query immediately

"At a glance" summary

A scannable block near the top that AI can extract as a self-contained answer

FAQ with schema markup

Structured Q&A sections with FAQPage schema for featured snippets and AI citations

Chunked sections

Content broken into clearly headed sections of ~400 words, each targeting a specific sub-query

Entity markup

Organization, Service, and LocalBusiness schema so AI systems understand what you do and who you serve

Internal linking

Topic clusters with authority pages linking to supporting long-tail pages, building topical depth
